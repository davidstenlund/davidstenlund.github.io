Sample course material

The material below provides a snapshot from a lecture in the course MATH 307 – Applied Linear Algebra, which I have taught a total of four times at UBC during 2021–2023.

1. Sample lecture plan

These are my handwritten notes with preparations for a 75 min lecture on the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT). In the lecture plan I would typically write down some questions for my students, and here I have also indicated the estimated time needed to go over the material, so that I can better keep track of the time and adjust the pacing if needed.

2. Video recording from actual lecture

This is a video recording of part of the lecture (ca. 10 min), including a demonstration of filtering out certain frequencies from a digital sound recording. The video file is compressed due to limits on file size.

3. Lecture notes

The lecture notes are always scanned and uploaded to the course webpage after the lecture. In this case, the final page was rather hastily sketched during the lecture, and therefore I rewrote that page a bit neater before scanning the notes.

4. Python example

A Jupyter notebook was used during the lecture to illustrate how to compute and modify the DFT using Python.
